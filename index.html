Screwdriver Belt Holder

After ordering a Screwdriver from Linus Tech Tips, I realized that I wanted a way to carry the screwdriver in a better way than simply holding it in my back pocket, as such I designed a holster that could clip to my belt and safely store the screwdriver while I'm not using it. The holster is designed to be 3D printed, with support only on the clip portion, a TPU sleeve is printed separately, and is designed to fit in the hard plastic holster with an interference fit below, you should see a 3D model of the completed project compiled in Solidworks Visualize and displayed here by use of the model-viewer package.

Modular Robotic Gripper Test Platform

While working in the Soft Robotics Lab at WPI I noticed a need for a reconfigurable gripper that would allow for the precise adjustment of the distance between fingers in radial grasps. To address this need I designed a radial gripper driven by a linear stage which allowed for precise adjustment of the gripper's radius. I was then able to select appropriate linear actuators and hardware to actuate the gripper.

Metamorphic manufacturing MQP 2021

As part of the Metamorphic Manufacturing V1 Team, I worked with my colleagues to initially research similar works and compiled a set of key features which would be vital to the project's success. I then worked to measure the required forces and material properties for the end-of-arm tooling required to successfully deform plasticine clay. This information was then used to select linear stages and lightweight materials. Once acquired I utilized my experience in 3D printing and manual machining to 3D print and manufacture interface plates to permit easy swapping of the system onto the robot. We were able to present our findings to colleagues and transfer our knowledge so the next team was able to build off our work.
